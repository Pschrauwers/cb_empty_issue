Maybe we don’t need keystone XL

Manufacturing in China isn’t as cheap as it used to be. An unexpected ‘reshoring’ revolution is bringing scores of jobs back home

From pocket squares to housewares, putting initials on stuff is turning into one of the biggest trends of 2013. (At JCrew.com’s new “monogram shop,” you can have a few letters stitched onto any cashmere knit or shirt pocket for $10.) Monograms never truly went out of style, which makes this particular bandwagon an easy one to climb aboard. And here’s the thing: you don’t need anyone’s approval to put your stamp on a favourite item. Nor do you need to use your own initials: TMI, WTF, S&P. You can get creative. If the point of a monogram is to make sure an article doesn’t get lost in a sea of blue button-downs, go ahead and make yours count.

—Chris Johns
